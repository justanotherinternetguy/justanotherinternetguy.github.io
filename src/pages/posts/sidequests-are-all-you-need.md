# Sidequests are All You Need

> Take chances, make mistakes, and get messy! - Ms. Frizzle

With the abundance of free time and the rather optional nature of maintaining a GPA as a high school senior, I have taken to doing _completely_ and utterly random shit. With the exception of a few days in the following weeks where I need to cram the entire AP Physics C Mechanics and E&M curricula, I have been doing everything _but_ schoolwork (much to the dismay of my teachers).

#

> sidequest [noun]: a part of a computer game that has its own aim and story but is not part of the main game - cambridge dictionary

I've been spending that time on exploring and generally _doing cool things_. From traveling out to hackathons and sneaking around UCLA's campus at 2am to getting lost in the woods, it's been an incredible past few months and I have no intention on stopping (especially since the summer break is rolling around).

I wanted to reflect on some of the shifts in mindset I've had in the past few months and some advice for others that just are looking to... explore.

# Overcommit! Say YES to EVERYTHING!

The greatest sidequests and opportunities come from just saying yes. You must carry a mindset that you _will_ succeed _regardless_ of what happens. You must believe you are capable of learning and doing _anything_ in _any_ span of time. -->
