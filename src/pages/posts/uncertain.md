#

> "No matter what happens, I'm sure it will turn out OK in the end" - my (now retired) high school counselor

#

During one particularly stressful meeting with my high school counselor in junior year, I expressed to her my doubts and confusion about the college application process. As with many other high schoolers in the same boat, I was terrified of choosing the wrong college, messing up my applications, writing bad essays, and the plethora of other concerns surrounding college applications.

While I forget the rest of the advice she gave me to comfort me, I remember her saying, very clearly, "No matter what happens, I'm sure it will turn out OK in the end." For some reason, this grand and overgeneralized statement was what calmed me down the most.

Looking back on my high school years, everything _did_ turn out OK in the end. I wasted time, I failed tests, I bombed out of competitions, I missed opportunities and deadlines. I have regrets -- many regrets -- on how I could have optimized high school or lived my life better. Yet, even with all these mistakes, things still worked out perfectly fine.
