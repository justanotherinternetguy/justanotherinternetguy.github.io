Naming conventions and designation

Tri-service aircraft system (Air Force/Army/Navy) 1962–present

The tri-service naming system was designed in 1962 to unify and standardize the naming conventions of the US Air Force, Navy, and Army.
The system uses the following template to construct names:

(Status Prefix) (Modified Mission) (Basic Mission) (Vehicle Type) - (Design Number) (Series Letter)

Status Prefix

  • e: Digitally developed
  • G: Permanently grounded
  • J: Special test, temporary
  • N: Special test, permanent
  • X: Experimental
  • Y: Prototype
  • Z: Planning

Modified Mission

  • A: Attack
  • C: Cargo (i.e., transport)
  • D: Drone director
  • E: Electronic warfare
  • F: Fighter
  • H: Search and rescue, MEDEVAC
  • K: Tanker
  • L: Modified for cold weather operations
  • M: Multi-mission (i.e., Special Operations)
  • O: Observation
  • P: Maritime patrol
  • Q: Unmanned aerial vehicle
  • R: Reconnaissance
  • S: Anti-submarine warfare
  • T: Trainer
  • U: Utility
  • V: VIP transport
  • W: Weather reconnaissance

Basic Mission

  • A: Attack aircraft (for tactical air-to-surface mission)
  • B: Bomber (for strategic air-to-surface mission)
  • C: Cargo (i.e. Transport)
  • E: Special electronic installation
  • F: Fighter
  • K: Tanker (dropped between 1977 and 1985)
  • L: Laser-equipped
  • O: Observation (Forward Air Control)
  • P: Maritime patrol
  • R: Reconnaissance
  • S: Anti-submarine warfare
  • T: Trainer
  • U: Utility
  • X: Special research

Vehicle Type

Not needed for most fixed-wing aircraft

  • D: Unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) control segment
  • G: Glider
  • H: Helicopter
  • Q: Unmanned aerial vehicle
  • S: Spaceplane
  • V: Vertical take-off/short take-off and landing (VTOL/STOL)
  • Z: Lighter-than-air
