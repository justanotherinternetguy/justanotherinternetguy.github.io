Lockheed R6V Constitution

Perhaps not many know about the R6V - only 2 prototypes were ever created and much of the plane has been lost to the annals of time. In many ways, the R6V, like most Lockheed planes, were cutting-edge and at the very forefront of aeronautical development for its time. The Constitution started in 1942 as a joint study between Lockheed, the U.S. Navy, and Pan Am. The Navy wanted a new transport aircraft to either replace or improve on its aging fleet of flying boats.

The "double bubble" fuselage, which has the cross-section of a "figure-eight," provided the same structural advantages of using a cylinder for full-cabin pressurization, but without the wasted space that came with a cylinder of similar volume. Such a design was first tested on the C-46 Commando, but the R6V was still very innovative for its time. The aircraft remains, to this day, the largest fixed-wing aircraft ever operated by the U.S. Navy.
