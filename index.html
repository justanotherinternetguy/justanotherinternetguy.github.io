    Fisher P-75 Eagle

    The Fisher P-75 Eagle was the actually the first plane in the USAF to be named "Eagle" (of course, the second one being the F-15). Using the same logic as the P-38 Lightning & F-35 Lightning II names, the F-15 Eagle should have been named the F-15 Eagle II.
    All that aside, the P-75 was designed to have an extremely high rate of climb in order to intercept enemy bombers during WW2. It used most powerful water-cooled piston engine available, an Allison V-3420. It used contra-rotating propellers to give it more stability and thrust. However, the USAF terminated the P-75 program after building only 14 models, instead favoring long-range bomber escorts such as the P-51 and P-38.
    In my opinion, it looks like a badly mutated mate of the P-51 and P-39.
